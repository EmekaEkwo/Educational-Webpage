What Is Small-Sided Soccer?

Small-sided soccer is any competitive match with fewer than 11 players per team—typically 3v3 to 8v8—played on a smaller field with smaller goals. The tighter space speeds up the game and encourages constant involvement.

It's great for kids who are learning and adults who want to develop quicker because the changing situations force fast decisions. You get more touches, more pressure, and more problem-solving than in full-size matches, which accelerates improvement in ball mastery and confidence.

Core Benefits at a Glance

  • More touches and repetitions per player
  • Faster decision-making in tight spaces
  • Inclusive, easy-to-organize games

How to Start Your First Game (3v3)

  1. Pick a format and set a small field (20–30m × 15–20m).
  2. Use small goals or cones; play short, intense halves.
  3. Kick-ins instead of throw-ins; optional no goalkeeper; quick restarts.
