What Is Small-Sided Soccer?

Smallsided soccer is any competetive match with less than 11 players, typically ranging from 3v3 to 8v8 teams where the feild and goals is decreased in size. Small sided soccer aids in the development of kids aswell as the refinement of adults, forcing both groups to constantly adapt to dynamic changing game scenarios

Players get far more touches on the ball than in larger scale games and are therefore exposed to more stimuli where the are forced to be creative. additionally due to smaller pitch size, players receive more touches on the ball compare to a larger scale game, aiding the development of the individuals overall ball mastery

Core Benefits at a Glance

  • More touches and repetitions per player
  • Faster decisions in tight spaces
  • Inclusive, easy-to-organize games

How to Start Your First Game (3v3)

  1. Pick a format (3v3 or 4v4) and set a 20-30 m x 15-20 m field.
  2. Use small goals or cones; play two halves (e.g., 7 minutes each).
  3. Basic rules: kick-ins instead of throw-ins, no goalkeepers (optional), quick restarts.
